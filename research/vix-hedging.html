Dynamic VIX-Based Hedging of Equity Risk during Market Stress Events
Period2024–2025 AffiliationFlorida State University Associated withIndependent Research · Portfolio Risk Management RoleIndividual project StatusCompleted
Risk management Portfolio hedging VIX derivatives Backtesting

Overview

Designed and backtested a dynamic volatility-based hedge for equity portfolios using VIX-linked instruments during macro-driven stress regimes. The strategy dynamically adjusts hedge ratios based on market regime identification and VIX term structure.

GitHub Write-up (PDF)

Contributions

  • Developed regime-switching model to identify stress periods and optimize hedge timing.
  • Implemented dynamic hedging using VIX calls and futures; backtested across multiple equity indices and crisis windows.
  • Analyzed trade-offs between hedge costs (basis risk) and protection; benchmarked against static hedges.

Methods & technical notes

  • Models: Markov regime-switching; VIX term structure; Greeks approximation.
  • Data: Equity prices, VIX, VIX futures, options chains; crisis windows: 2008, 2020, etc.
  • Performance: Sharpe ratio, max drawdown, portfolio delta in stress scenarios.

Results

  • Dynamic hedge reduced maximum drawdown by ~15–25% vs unhedged during crisis periods.
  • Cost-benefit analysis: hedge ratio optimization balances premium paid vs tail risk protection.
