Schubert Calculus and the Cohomology of the Grassmannian
Period2021 AffiliationLahore University of Management Sciences (LUMS) Associated withUndergraduate Thesis · Algebraic Geometry RoleThesis author
Algebraic geometry Intersection theory Schubert calculus Grassmannian

Overview

Conducted undergraduate thesis research on intersection theory and Schubert calculus, culminating in a defended thesis presentation. Explored the cohomology ring of Grassmannians and applications to enumerative geometry.

Thesis (PDF)

Contributions

  • Studied Schubert varieties, Schubert classes, and their intersection multiplicities on Grassmannians.
  • Developed computational techniques for computing Schubert structure constants (Littlewood–Richardson coefficients).
  • Applied theory to classic enumerative problems (e.g., lines in space, osculating flags).

Methods & technical notes

  • Theory: Schubert varieties, cohomology, Weyl groups, root systems.
  • Computation: Young tableaux, Littlewood–Richardson rule, combinatorial algorithms.
  • Applications: Enumerative geometry examples; classical geometry connections.

Results

  • Comprehensive introduction to Schubert calculus with worked examples.
  • Thesis defended successfully; received commendation for clarity and rigor.

Notes

Thesis spans ~60 pages with carefully designed diagrams. Math: the cohomology is $H^* \text{Gr}(k, n) \cong \mathbb{Z}[c_1, \ldots, c_k]$ where $c_i$ are Chern classes, and $[\sigma_\lambda] \cap [\sigma_\mu] = c_{\lambda + \mu}$ in suitable sense.
