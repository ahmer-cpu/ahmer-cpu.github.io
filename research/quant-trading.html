How these metrics are computed

All metrics are computed on simple (not log) returns from a dollar-neutral long/short portfolio, indexed by trading day. Annualization assumes 252 trading days per year. Transaction costs modeled at 10 bps per trade.

Jensen's alpha. Annualized excess return unexplained by market beta: $$\alpha = \left[(\bar{r}_s - r_f) - \beta \cdot (\bar{r}_b - r_f)\right] \cdot N$$ where $\bar{r}_s$ and $\bar{r}_b$ are mean daily strategy and benchmark returns, $\beta = \text{Cov}(r_s, r_b) / \text{Var}(r_b)$, and $N = 252$.
Sharpe ratio. Excess return per unit of total volatility: $$\text{SR} = \frac{\bar{r} - r_f}{\sigma} \cdot \sqrt{N}$$
Newey-West t-statistic. Corrects for autocorrelation in overlapping IC series: $$t_{\text{NW}} = \frac{\bar{\text{IC}}}{\hat{\sigma}_{\text{NW}} / \sqrt{T}}, \qquad \hat{\sigma}^2_{\text{NW}} = \hat{\gamma}_0 + 2\sum_{j=1}^{L}\left(1 - \frac{j}{L+1}\right)\hat{\gamma}_j$$ where $L = h - 1$ (horizon minus one) and $\hat{\gamma}_j$ are autocovariances. Without this correction, overlapping 21-day returns inflate t-statistics by ~3.5×.
Block bootstrap 95% CI. Resamples IC series in blocks of length $h$ to preserve serial dependence, computes the mean IC for each of $B = 10{,}000$ resamples, and reports the 2.5th and 97.5th percentiles.
Maximum drawdown. Largest peak-to-trough decline: $$\text{MDD} = \min_t \left( \frac{V_t - \max_{\tau \leq t} V_\tau}{\max_{\tau \leq t} V_\tau} \right)$$
CVaR (Conditional Value at Risk). Expected loss in the worst $\alpha$% of days: $$\text{CVaR}_\alpha = \mathbb{E}[r \mid r \leq \text{VaR}_\alpha]$$
